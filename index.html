Introduction

The Beginning of Human Data

长期以来，AI的成功依赖大规模数据和算力的scaling。不像自动驾驶和大语言模型领域，具身智能的一个非常重要的区别是没有存量数据，也很难在商业化的运营中自然的积累数据。因此，怎么scaling数据成了整个具身智能领域最重要的问题。人类日常工作生活中一直在使用双臂双手进行灵巧操作，一个最直接的想法是将这些数据作为具身智能的数据燃料。但是，使用人类数据进行模型训练长期面临着两个问题，一是由于人手和机器人存在embodiment gap，直接将人手数据用于模型训练面临运动学和动力学与机器人不一致的问题。二是目前人类数据大部分来源于网络上的第一人称视频，又或者少量的开源数据集，数量和质量一直没有形成一个规模化后的结果。如何对人类数据更好地利用和研究，以及规模化后的结果一直是个悬而未决的问题。我们提出的Psi-R2和Psi-W0是第一个使用10W小时量级人类数据预训练的具身模型。接下来我们会详细介绍一下Psi-R2和Psi-W0的设计和一些思考，以及它们各自的作用。

Introduction

The Beginning of Human Data

For a long time, AI success has depended on scaling massive data and compute. Unlike autonomous driving and large language models, embodied intelligence differs in one very important way: there is no large stockpile of existing data, and it is also difficult to accumulate data naturally through commercial operations. As a result, how to scale data has become the most important problem in the entire embodied AI field. In daily work and life, humans constantly use two arms and two hands for dexterous manipulation, so the most direct idea is to use such data as fuel for embodied intelligence. But training models with human data has long faced two problems. First, because there is an embodiment gap between human hands and robots, directly using human-hand data for model training creates inconsistencies in kinematics and dynamics. Second, most human data currently comes either from first-person videos on the internet or from a small number of open-source datasets, and neither quantity nor quality has yet produced a truly scaled result. How to better use and study human data, and what happens after scaling, has remained an open question.

Using human data to supplement real-robot data has been a major research focus in recent years, and how to use it for pretraining has been one of the main research directions. Our X framework is the first model pretrained with broadly collected human data at the scale of 100,000 hours. Our framework contains two models, Psi-R2 and Psi-W0, together with an evaluation, improvement, and flywheel loop that connects them. Next, I will explain the design of the X framework and some of our thinking in detail.

Chapter 1

1. Psi-R2：What Matters in Learning from Human Data

Psi-R2的输入是图片和语言，输出是预测的未来视频和机器人的动作。整个框架的backbone是建立在预训练的视频生成模型上的，因此，我们的Psi-R2和最近比较火的WAM的定义一致，同时我们输入有图片和语言，输出有动作，因此也算是VLA，只不过backbone是一个视频生成模型，这两者的概念是不冲突的。

Psi-R2训练使用使用的bachbone是开源的Wan2.2-IT2V-5B-480P，训练时联合预测未来视频帧 + 机器人动作。我们在于训练阶段同时使用机器人数据和人类数据，机器人数据全部是使用的灵初的Psi-MobiDex，总共5417小时。人类数据包含使用灵初自研的外骨骼手套采集的数据加上不戴手套的采集的裸手人类数据，总共95472小时，包含74294种场景，4821种任务，1382种物体。通过预训练后，我们可以用非常少的真机数据微调（<100条轨迹），就能完成非常长程，精细的操作，包括装配手机，工业包装，叠纸盒等。

对于人类数据和真机数据之间的处理，我们选择了非常简单的做法：直接将人类的关节通过运动学和机器人对齐，图片不做任何处理，然后直接输入到模型里面作为State和Action Label。因此，我们研究的是如何使用人类数据来帮助某一本体进行预训练，而不是训练出一个能够跨本体使用的模型--前者才是真正帮助机器人商业化的核心，而后者仍然是一个研究性的问题。

在我们研究的过程中，尝试过非常多种对人类数据做特殊处理的方法，包括图像inpainting，keypoint辅助loss，尝试将人和机器人数据在某一空间对齐等等。但到最后我们选择相信The Bitter Lesson：Raw Data In, Raw Data Out。因为我们发现，当数据量足够大时，所有的人为设计的模块都会成为性能的瓶颈。当我们使用那些让人类数据更贴近机器人数据的方法后，虽然在一些简单任务上比如pick and place更容易训了，但是在一些长程的，精细的操作上反而是有害的--因为大部分这些方法的本质是想模糊人类和机器人的数据，让模型尽量认为这两种数据是一样的，从而更好地学到一些共性。但两者的dynamic其实是有差距的，对于比较精细的操作来说，更难区分这两者就意味着更容易有误差，最后导致任务失败。所以，我们之后没有做更多的人为设计，而是在做一层输入输出维度对齐后直接让模型自己从海量的数据当中学习。最后的发现也非常有意思，当总体数据量比较小时效果不明显，但当数据量足够大后，我们发现简单的设计往往带来更好的效果，直接使用原始数据输入和输出的泛化性，长程能力和操作上限是最高的。

在整个研究的过程中，我们发现影响模型性能更重要的是对数据，特别是对人类数据的理解。这里面包含了数据的配比，Scheduler，Curriculum，Retrieval，and Evaluation。怎么设计训练输入的比例和阶段，怎么retrieve pair data，怎么eval人类数据的知识已经真正注入到了机器人模型里面，都是影响模型性能真正的关键。这里面有不少是需要利用Psi-W0这种Action-Condition的World Model的能力的，特别是对策略的评估，能评估才能提升，才能证明人类数据的知识真的被学到了。这也是为什么我们同时训练了Psi-W0，详细的做法会在第二章说明。

同时，整个数据管线也非常重要，包括对数据的清洗，标注，质检，审核等功能。我们所有的数据质检和标注都使用Auto Labeling的方式完成，只在最后需要人工审核。同时在数据的质检中引入Psi-W0，运用世界模型的可视化能力为数据质量打分。在整套Pipeline的设计一开始，我们就希望人类数据不只是从image中提供一些高层次的语义信息，而是包括所有的操作细节，所以我们会让人类数据的3D轨迹尽可能准确。从纯Ego视频数据中恢复人手操作的误差基本是厘米级的，但是借助我们灵初的外骨骼手套，可以将其降低到亚毫米级的精度。举个例子，我们会在数据收集前使用数采手套进行机器人的遥操作来调整参数，确保其映射的准确。在数据管线中，也还会再对漂移的或者不准的pose进行修正，确保最后使用到的数据都是非常精确，可在机器人上replay出来的轨迹。对于数据的详细分析可以看第三章。最后，WAM的一个痛点是推理慢，我们也花费了非常多的工程努力，包括DiT Caching，Torch Compile，Quantization等，将其从单次推理2.2s降低到了100ms以下，保证了操作时灵巧与顺滑。

Chapter 1

1. Psi-R2: What Matters in Learning from Human Data

Psi-R2 takes images and language as input and outputs predicted future video together with robot actions. The backbone of the entire framework is built on a pretrained video-generation model. In that sense, our X model matches the recent definition of WAM. At the same time, because we input images and language and output actions, it can also be called a VLA. The difference is simply that the backbone happens to be a video-generation model; these two ideas are not in conflict.

Psi-R2 uses the open-source Wan2.2-IT2V-5B-480P as its backbone and is trained to jointly predict future video frames and robot actions. During training, we use both robot data and human data. The robot data all come from Lingchu's MobiDex, for a total of 5,417 hours. The human data include data collected with Lingchu's self-developed exoskeleton glove plus bare-hand human data collected without gloves, totaling 95,472 hours and covering X scenes, X tasks, and X objects. After pretraining, we can fine-tune with very little real-robot data (less than 10 hours) and still complete very long-horizon and fine-grained operations, including phone assembly, industrial packaging, and folding paper boxes.

In handling the relationship between human data and real-robot data, we chose a very simple approach: directly align human joints with the robot through kinematics, do no processing to the images, and then feed everything into the model as state and action labels. As a result, what we study is how human data can help pretrain a model for one specific embodiment, rather than how to train a model that can be used across embodiments. The former is what truly matters for robot commercialization; the latter is still a research problem.

In our research process, we tried many kinds of special treatment for human data, including image inpainting, keypoint-assisted losses, and attempts to align human and robot data in some intermediate space. In the end, however, we chose to trust The Bitter Lesson: raw data in, raw data out. We found that once data volume becomes large enough, every hand-designed module turns into a performance bottleneck. When we used methods intended to make human data look closer to robot data, training became easier on simple tasks such as pick and place, but these methods actually hurt long-horizon and fine-grained manipulation. Most of these methods try to blur the difference between human and robot data so that the model treats them as the same and learns shared patterns more easily. But their dynamics are in fact different. For precise manipulation, making the two harder to distinguish means larger error and ultimately more task failures. So later we stopped adding more hand-designed processing. After only aligning the input and output dimensions, we let the model learn directly from massive data on its own. The result was also interesting: when the total data amount was small, the effect was unclear, but once the data became large enough, simpler design consistently worked better. Directly using raw inputs and outputs produced the best generalization, the strongest long-horizon ability, and the highest performance ceiling.

Throughout the project, we found that the most important factor for model performance was understanding the data, especially human data. That includes data ratios, scheduler, curriculum, retrieval, and evaluation. How to design the input ratio and stages of training, how to retrieve paired data, and how to evaluate whether human-data knowledge has truly been injected into the robot model are what really determine performance. Many of these pieces require the capabilities of Psi-W0 as an action-conditioned world model, especially policy evaluation. Only if you can evaluate can you improve, and only then can you prove that the knowledge from human data has actually been learned. This is also why we trained Psi-W0 alongside Psi-R2; the detailed method will be explained in Chapter 2.

At the same time, the entire data pipeline is also crucial, including data cleaning, annotation, quality inspection, and review. All of our data inspection and labeling are completed through AutoLabeling, with human review needed only at the final stage. We also introduce Psi-W0 into the quality-inspection process and use the visualizability of the world model to score data quality. From the start of the pipeline design, we wanted human data to contribute not only high-level semantic information from images, but also all manipulation details, so we tried to make the 3D trajectories of human data as accurate as possible. Recovering hand motion from pure ego-video is usually only centimeter-level accurate, but with Lingchu's exoskeleton glove we can reduce that to sub-millimeter precision. For example, before data collection we use the glove to teleoperate the robot and adjust parameters so that the mapping is accurate. In the data pipeline, we also correct drifting or inaccurate poses again so that the final data used are all highly precise trajectories that can be replayed on the robot. For a more detailed analysis of the data, see Chapter 3. Finally, one pain point of WAM is slow inference. We spent a great deal of engineering effort on DiT caching, Torch Compile, quantization, and more, bringing single-pass inference from 2.2 seconds to below 100 ms and ensuring dexterity and smoothness during manipulation.

2. Psi-W0: Do RL in the World Model

Psi-W0的输入是图片和语言和机器人的Action轨迹，输出是未来预测的视频。整个框架的backbone也是建立在预训练的视频生成模型上的，但和Psi-R2最大的区别是Action是作为输入，来控制输出的Video。因此，大部分人也把其叫做Action-Conditioned World Model (AC-WM)。

可能有人会问，我们已经可以用Psi-R2来预测未来的Video了，为什么还需要一个AC-WM？这里面的核心在于一个点：反事实（Artifact）。Psi-R2的所有训练数据都必须是有目的且成功的，所以其无法生成失败的预测--而这些反事实对于策略学习，特别是强化学习来说是非常重要的。因此，Psi-W0一个更重要的功能是能够对Policy进行评估，提升，与形成飞轮，这里面的核心是强化学习。

Psi-W0的训练也是使用Wan2.2-IT2V-5B-480P，与Psi-R2相同的数据形式。但是在Psi-R2使用的数据量的基础上，还有大概额外30%的专门收集或正常数采/推理中产生的失败数据，来确保对反事实的精准建模。

Psi-W0的主要作用有两个，一是在Psi-R2训练过程中进行评估，在训练时将Psi-R2在某些人类数据的场景中进行Rollout，评估策略模型是否真的学到了人类数据的知识。二是更核心的，将人类数据转换成机器人数据。这个是如何做的呢，最早的思路早在我们24年这篇paper《Object-Centric Dexterous Manipulation from Human Motion Data》这里就有了：将人类数据用于机器人上，最核心是用强化学习将人类数据的dynamic迁移到机器人的dynamic上。打个比方，比如都是抓一个苹果这个任务，用人类抓苹果的数据通过运动学retarget到机器人上时，大概率会由于embodiement gap导致直接通过运动学转换出来的机器人的角度是不完全精准的，但通常只会有一点点小的偏差。

因此，最直接的想法便是拿强化学习做一层微调，令其转换后的机器人轨迹能够物理上合理地拿起这个苹果。以前的做法是将物体reconstruct进仿真器里做强化学习，但是这一套太heavy了，并且由于Sim2Real Gap难以模仿可变形的物体。因此，现在我们将这一套Pipeline中的仿真器用AC-WM来代替，假如我们有训练好的一个Psi-R2和Psi-W0，在我们拿Psi-R2学习完这一条人类数据之后我们将其输入进Psi-W0中进行Rollout，然后将Psi-W0的visual和dynamic切换成机器人的模式，再通过强化学习的方法对其进行一些微调，使其在符合机器人dynamic的基础上能够完成着一个人类数据的任务。这样我们就将这条人类数据的知识吸收进了Policy里面，提升了模型的能力，当然也产生了新的数据。之后，我们可以将里面好的数据筛选出来，重新加入Psi-R2和Psi-W0的训练，形成数据飞轮。还可以对Psi-W0进行一些随机化，形成新的场景，生成新的数据。

所以，Psi-W0的功能和Psi-R2不一样，他们的目标不是完成任务，而是能够对一个策略进行评估，提升，并同时将人类数据转换成机器人数据。所以，Psi-W0需要的数据不仅是成功完成任务的数据，更需要的是失败的，无意义的数据，让Policy能够在AC-WM里面进行强化学习训练。

2. Psi-W0: Do RL in the World Model

Psi-W0 takes images, language, and robot action trajectories as input and outputs predicted future video. Its backbone is also built on a pretrained video-generation model. The biggest difference from Psi-R2 is that action is used as an input in order to control the output video. For this reason, most people would call it an action-conditioned world model (AC-WM).

Some people may ask: if Psi-R2 can already predict future video, why do we still need an AC-WM? The core issue here is one thing: counterfactuals. All training data for Psi-R2 must be intentional and successful, so it cannot generate failed predictions. But those counterfactual outcomes are extremely important for policy learning, especially for reinforcement learning. Because of that, one of Psi-W0's more important functions is to evaluate policies, improve them, and help form a flywheel, with reinforcement learning at the center.

Psi-W0 is also trained with Wan2.2-IT2V-5B-480P and uses the same data format as Psi-R2. But on top of the amount of data used by Psi-R2, it also includes roughly an additional 30% of specially collected failure data or failures produced during normal collection and inference, so that counterfactuals can be modeled accurately.

Psi-W0 serves two main purposes. The first is evaluation during Psi-R2 training: while training, Psi-R2 is rolled out in some scenes from human data to check whether the policy model has really learned the knowledge contained in human data. The second, and more central one, is converting human data into robot data. How is this done? The earliest idea can be traced back to the 2024 paper Object-Centric Dexterous Manipulation from Human Motion Data: the key to using human data on robots is to use reinforcement learning to transfer human dynamics into robot dynamics. For example, take the task of grasping an apple. If we kinematically retarget human apple-grasping data to the robot, the embodiment gap will very likely make the converted robot joint angles imprecise, though usually the error is only a small offset.

So the most direct idea is to do one layer of reinforcement-learning fine-tuning so that the converted robot trajectory can physically and plausibly pick up the apple. Earlier methods reconstructed the object in a simulator and did reinforcement learning there, but that pipeline is too heavy, and because of the sim-to-real gap it is especially hard to model deformable objects. We now replace the simulator in this pipeline with an AC-WM. Suppose we already have a trained Psi-R2 and Psi-W0. After Psi-R2 has learned from one human-data trajectory, we feed it into Psi-W0 for rollout, switch Psi-W0's visual and dynamic mode to the robot domain, and then apply reinforcement-learning fine-tuning so that under robot dynamics it can still complete the human-data task. In this way, the knowledge from that human trajectory is absorbed into the policy, the model becomes stronger, and new data are also generated. After that, we can filter out the good data and add them back into Psi-R2 and Psi-W0 training to form a data flywheel. We can also randomize Psi-W0 to create new scenes and generate new data.

So Psi-W0 is functionally different from Psi-R2. Its goal is not simply to complete tasks. Its goal is to evaluate and improve a policy while also converting human data into robot data. That means Psi-W0 needs not only data from successful task completion, but also failed and even meaningless data, so that the policy can undergo reinforcement-learning training inside the AC-WM.

3. What Really Determines Data Value

人类数据用来做模型训练早在具身的早期阶段就有，但是最近是特别兴起的一波风潮。在这过程中，也出现了五花八门的数据收集方法。在设备侧，有直接戴个相机在头上就开始录的，有需要用更精准的光学追踪人类身上各个位置的，也有戴着手套采集触觉信息的。采集方式也有很多，有在in the wild不同地方采的，有带移动同时操作的，有在一个个小房间里采的，也有在固定的数据采集厂采的。那这里面到底什么样的数据才是对预训练有帮助的呢。这是一个很难有严格证明的东西，但是我们通过几个月的研究，发现了一些方法。先说结论，在我们的模型训练中，我们发现信噪比是真正影响人类数据能否用在预训练中的关键。信噪比低的，可能对于训练反而是副作用。那么如何判断信噪比呢，我们发现，对于数据集本身的分布来说，操作任务多样性>物体多样性>>场景多样性，而对于模态来说，精准的3D位姿>>触觉>2D图像特征。

对于数据集，我们发现场景多样性的重要程度是最低的，借助预训练的视频生成模型，不管是Policy还是World我们都能很轻易的实现跨背景泛化，哪怕训练数据的背景多样性并不高或几乎没有。在我们的实验中，不管是视频生成任务还是策略操作任务，如果使用预训练的Wan的参数，模型很轻易地就能在未见过的场景中进行泛化，如果不使用则很容易失败。因此，场景的泛化很大程度上来源于视频生成模型。对于操作来说，操作永远都是object-centric的，所有的任务都能被建模成我们需要操作哪一个物体，需要怎么操作这个物体，因此物体的多样性是非常重要的。而对于一个预训练的数据集来说，这里面质量的核心永远是任务的多样性，才能cover得住各种各样的下游任务。在我们的实验中，任务的泛化是最难的，并且呈现很明显的预训练见过的任务越多，跨任务的泛化能力更强。所以，我们人类数据数据集的构建最需要的是频繁更换任务和物体，而背景是最不重要的。

对于模态，我们倾向于尽可能地收集多的模态，然后让模型在数据中自己挖掘出信息。因此，我们的输入里面除了常规的语言，视频，机器人关节，还有覆盖全手的织物触觉。而这里面最核心的其实是整个人手的3D位置的追踪，这代表了我们将2D的视频生成模型往3D空间的操作模型走的最重要的补充信息。因此，我们花了大量的精力在优化这部分上，包括用更准的传感器，误差的后补偿等。我们是怎么验证这一点的呢，借助Psi-W0，其实我们有一个很好的方法。我们将做同一任务不同精度的设备录制的3D轨迹通过上面第二章说的流程来进行人类数据到机器人数据的转换，让其在Psi-W0中生成对应的机器人操作的数据。最后很明显，使用灵初手套采集的3D轨迹转换成机器人轨迹时的效果最好，因为其本身就和机器人的dynamic更align。但是使用裸手通过CV估算的轨迹则误差特别大，甚至完全无法完成任务。

触觉详细的第五章再讨论，而2D图像特征（keypoint，mask等）更像是之前说的人为设计的模块，在大数据量之后对于模型基本没有帮助，所以我们在最后甚至都没有用这一部分。因此，对于人类数据，我们其实认为有两类。一类是力求精准的，整个轨迹在机器人空间下的，能够在处理后能直接和机器人数据等效/在机器人上Replay出来的数据。这种数据是有可能能直接用来做后训练代替机器人数据的。一个代表的便是使用灵初数采手套，在严格条件下采集的数据。还有一类是注重泛化的，舍弃精确度拥抱scalability的，这种数据可能永远无法用来直接做后训练，但能非常快的上量并给预训练模型提供很强的泛化能力，这两种我认为都是非常重要的。

Chapter 3

3. Signal-to-noise ratio: what really determines data value

Human data were used for model training even in the early stages of embodied AI, but recently this has become a particularly strong wave. In the process, many different data-collection methods have appeared. On the hardware side, some simply mount a camera on the head and start recording; some require more precise optical tracking of various positions on the human body; some use gloves to collect tactile signals. Collection settings also vary widely: some are collected in the wild, some involve mobility together with manipulation, some are collected in small rooms, and some in fixed data-collection factories. So what kind of data actually helps pretraining? It is hard to prove this rigorously, but after several months of study we found some patterns. The short answer is that, in our training, signal-to-noise ratio is what truly determines whether human data can help pretraining. Low-SNR data may even hurt training. How do we judge SNR? For dataset distribution, we found task diversity > object diversity >> scene diversity. For modalities, precise 3D pose >> tactile > 2D image features.

For datasets, we found that scene diversity is the least important factor. With pretrained video-generation models, both policy and world models can generalize across backgrounds quite easily, even when training data have little or almost no background diversity. In our experiments, whether for video generation or policy manipulation, if we used pretrained Wan parameters the model could generalize to unseen scenes easily; without them it failed much more often. So scene generalization comes largely from the video-generation model. For manipulation itself, everything is object-centric. Every task can be modeled as what object we need to manipulate and how we need to manipulate it, so object diversity matters a lot. But for a pretraining dataset, the core of quality is always task diversity, because only that can cover a wide range of downstream tasks. In our experiments, task generalization was the hardest part, and the effect was very clear: the more tasks seen during pretraining, the stronger cross-task generalization became. So when building a human-data dataset, what we most need is to change tasks and objects frequently, while background is the least important factor.

For modalities, we prefer to collect as many as possible and let the model discover the useful information on its own from data. So in addition to language, video, and robot joints, our inputs also include fabric-based tactile sensing covering the whole hand. The real core, however, is full-hand 3D pose tracking. This is the most important extra information for moving from a 2D video-generation model toward a 3D manipulation model. We therefore spent a great deal of effort improving this part, including better sensors and post-hoc compensation of error. How did we verify this? Psi-W0 gives us a good method. We take 3D trajectories of the same task recorded by devices with different precision, convert them from human data into robot data using the process described in Chapter 2, and let Psi-W0 generate the corresponding robot-manipulation data. The result is very clear: 3D trajectories collected by the Lingchu glove convert into robot trajectories best, because they are more aligned with robot dynamics from the start. By contrast, trajectories estimated from bare hands using computer vision have very large error and sometimes cannot complete the task at all.

Tactile data will be discussed in detail in Chapter 5. As for 2D image features such as keypoints and masks, they are closer to the kind of hand-designed modules mentioned earlier. At large scale, they provide almost no help to the model, so in the end we did not even use them. As a result, we think there are actually two kinds of human data. One kind pursues precision: trajectories in robot space that, after processing, can become directly equivalent to robot data or be replayed on the robot. Such data may be able to replace robot data during post-training. A representative example is the strictly collected data obtained with Lingchu's data-collection glove. The other kind focuses on generalization, giving up precision in exchange for scalability. These data may never be usable directly for post-training, but they can scale up very quickly and provide strong generalization ability to a pretrained model. We believe both are very important.

Chapter 4

4. Human Data or Not Human Data，That is the Question

很多人对于人类数据经常会抱有2个问题：1是我们是否不需要人类数据，真机遥操作数据如果到了某天足够多的话是否也可以直接全部拿真机数据进行训练。2是纯使用人类数据不加入任何真机数据是否也能够直接训出能用的模型。这两个问题在我看来其实是同一个回答：对于真实的商业化场景落地来说，纯人类数据能够训练出模型很可能是必须的。

商业化落地和在实验室做demo不太一样，里面最核心的一个点其实是在实验室中很多人忽略了的一个指标：节拍。对于一个作业的工人来说，他的SOP是经过了千锤百炼打磨之后的结果，一定是最优的。在一个工厂里可能某个工种的动作相比之前多了一步，整个节拍慢了一点，那就是几千万的损失。很多人在做demo时做不到的动作就换一个任务，或者换一个能完成任务的但是流程更长的方式来做，但是这在落地时往往都是不可能的。因此，最有价值的数据往往是真实场景作业工人的数据。

那人类数据怎么解决这个问题呢，1是人类数据本身的采集和工人的工作就完美贴合，比如让工人戴着一个手套工作，或者超市收银员戴着一个手套收银。这样作为数据采集的工具不仅不需要专门的数采员了，成本会降到最低，而且采集到的数据都是一线的最真实的作业数据，往往就是落地需要的。2是更关键的：人类数据的节拍能达到遥操作到不了但是机械臂运动的上限。由于遥操作需要操作员盯着机器人操作物体，因此大部分时间都比较慢--假设机械臂本身的运动上限是1200，遥操作很有可能只有800，甚至更低。而人类可以直接按着1200的速度来采集数据，因为这是他们的工作。因此收集到的数据从落地的角度看，反而质量可能是更高的--因为他们贴合实际sop和速度，这些可能是靠纯遥操作永远也达不到的。

那么，如何将这些快节拍的高质量数据训入模型里面就成了非常重要的能力，也就意味着纯使用人类数据能够训练出模型是很重要的。这其实和我们当年做Sim2Real非常像，我们验证一个仿真数据可不可用，需要做Zero-Shot Sim2Real。现在验证人类数据可不可用，也需要做Zero-Shot Human2Robot，只不过仿真器换成了世界模型。因此，这部分需要Psi-R2和Psi-W0的共同结合，借助上一章说的数据转换能力，将人类数据转换成机器人的数据。在我们自己的实验当中，我们尝试过直接使用转换完的人类数据来代替机器人数据做后训练，在一些简单的pick and place或者抓袋子任务中能看到不错的效果。但是，对于一些更精细的任务比如手机装配，插盒子等，成功率依然不是很高。这条路径仍然需要一定的时间研究，但我们认为是必须要做的。

Chapter 4

4. Human Data or Not Human Data, That is the Question

People often raise two questions about human data. First: maybe we do not need human data at all. If real-robot teleoperation data become plentiful enough one day, could we simply train entirely on real-robot data? Second: can we train a usable model using only human data, without any real-robot data at all? In my view these two questions actually have the same answer: for real commercial deployment, the ability to train models from pure human data may very well be necessary.

Commercial deployment is not the same as making demos in the lab. The most important issue is an indicator that many people ignore in the lab: takt time. For a worker on a real production line, the SOP is the result of repeated refinement and is necessarily the optimized one. In a factory, if one job type adds just one extra motion or slows the rhythm slightly, the loss can be in the tens of millions. In demo settings, if one behavior cannot be completed, people often switch to another task or use a longer but easier workflow that still finishes the job. But that is often impossible in deployment. As a result, the most valuable data are often the data from workers performing real tasks in real scenarios.

So how do human data solve this? First, human-data collection can align perfectly with the worker's actual job, for example by having a worker wear a glove while working, or a cashier wear a glove while handling checkout. As a data-collection tool, this means no special data collector is needed, the cost becomes minimal, and the collected data are the most authentic frontline operation data, which are often exactly what deployment needs. Second, and more importantly, human-data rhythm can reach the upper limit of arm motion that teleoperation often cannot. Because teleoperation requires the operator to watch the robot manipulate objects, it is usually much slower. If the mechanical limit of the robot arm is 1200, teleoperation may only reach 800, or even less. Humans, however, can collect data directly at 1200 speed because that is their real work. From a deployment perspective, the collected data may actually be higher quality because they reflect the real SOP and real speed, which pure teleoperation may never reach.

Therefore, the ability to train such high-tempo, high-quality data into a model becomes extremely important, and that also means it is very important to be able to train a model from pure human data. This is very similar to what we used to do with sim-to-real. To validate whether simulated data are usable, we need zero-shot sim-to-real. Now, to validate whether human data are usable, we also need zero-shot human-to-robot, except that the simulator has been replaced by a world model. That means Psi-R2 and Psi-W0 need to work together, using the data-conversion ability described in the previous chapter to convert human data into robot data. In our own experiments, we have tried directly replacing robot data in post-training with converted human data. On some simple tasks, such as pick and place or grasping bags, we can see good effects. But for finer tasks such as phone assembly and box insertion, the success rate is still not high. This route still requires more research time, but we believe it is something that must be done.

5. Tactile: A Medium for Cross-Embodiment Transfer

从直觉来看，触觉对于人类的操作无疑是非常重要的。但是为什么现在最先进的大脑模型上基本都没有怎么输入触觉模态呢，主要还是带触觉的真机数据太少了。现实中集成到真机上面的触觉传感器很多都比较不稳定，再加上各个传感器的格式都不太一样，因此触觉数据非常难scale up。但是对于人类数据来说，收集成本基本是机器人的1/10以下，并且相对而言设备比较便携，因此让我们有机会可以在人类数据的层面上scale up触觉，帮助我们模型的训练。

为什么我们认为对于人类数据来说触觉模态比较重要呢，因为操作的核心永远是contact，对于人类数据和机器人数据来说，他们长的看上去可能不一样，关节的构型导致的运动学动力学可能不一样，但是对于操作时碰没碰到物体的信号其实是一样的。因此，我们认为触觉会对人手到机械手的迁移起到很重要的作用，所以在设计数采手套产品时也把触觉模态的数据采集加上了，希望能借助触觉的信息将人手和机械手之间的dynamic gap更好地在世界模型中做一个补偿。

由于真机上的触觉数据格式和手套的大部分都不太一样，并且大部分机器人上面其实没有触觉传感器，因此我们直接使用Mask training的方法，在真机数据输入时即选择直接mask掉触觉传感器，并且是将触觉作为Action Label让模型进行预测而不是观测。最后从结果也可以看到，加入了触觉之后世界模型的能力远好于没加之前，并且对于机器人与物体的交互也有了一定预测的能力，说明其学会了碰撞这一信息。

5. What is tactile actually useful for: a medium for cross-embodiment transfer

Intuitively, tactile sensing is obviously very important for human manipulation. But why do the most advanced "brain" models today usually take little or no tactile modality as input? The main reason is still that there is far too little real-robot data with tactile sensing. In practice, many tactile sensors integrated on real robots are unstable, and the formats differ from sensor to sensor, so tactile data are very hard to scale up. Human data are different: the collection cost is typically below one tenth of robotic collection, and the devices are relatively portable, which gives us a chance to scale tactile sensing at the human-data level and use it to help train our models.

Why do we think tactile modality matters for human data? Because the core of manipulation is always contact. Human data and robot data may look different, and their joint configurations may imply different kinematics and dynamics, but the signal indicating whether contact with an object happened is fundamentally the same. For that reason, we believe tactile sensing can play an important role in transferring from the human hand to the robot hand. That is why, when designing our data-collection glove, we also included tactile-modality collection. We hope to use tactile information to better compensate for the dynamic gap between human hands and robot hands inside the world model.

Because tactile data on real robots usually have very different formats from those on the glove, and because most robots in practice do not have tactile sensors at all, we directly use mask training. When real-robot data are used as input, we simply mask out the tactile sensors. We also treat tactile signals as action labels to be predicted by the model rather than as observations. From the results, we can see that after adding tactile signals the world model is much stronger than before, and it also acquires some ability to predict robot-object interaction, showing that it has learned collision-related information.
